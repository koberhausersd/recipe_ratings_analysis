# Recipes Analysis – What Actually Constitutes “Healthy”?

*Author: Kate Oberhauser*

## Introduction
> “Eat food. Not too much. Mostly plants.” — Michael Pollan

In 2007, journalist Michael Pollan offered this simple advice as a guide to healthy eating. However, today’s nutrition guidance often feels far more complicated and contradictory. Each year seems to bring a new food fad: the Atkins diet, the high-fat, low-carb ketogenic diet, and our current age of protein, with protein now added to products from cereal and chips to coffee and even Pop-Tarts. As different macronutrients rise and fall in popularity, the definition of what counts as “healthy” becomes increasingly blurry amid the constant churn of dietary trends.

With this in mind, this project investigates what actually makes a recipe healthy and how a recipe’s healthiness relates to the nutrients it contains. I examine whether certain nutrients are more strongly associated with healthy recipes, or whether combinations of nutrients better explain these patterns. To do so I am analyzing two datasets of recipes and ratings from food.com.
